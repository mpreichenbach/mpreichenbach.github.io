As of February 2021, I work as a Research Mathematician at the Geospatial Research Laboratory, one of seven research laboratories of the Engineer Reseach Development Center in the US Army Corps of Engineers. I develop deep-learning models to do land-cover classification of high-resolution imagery, and create data analysis tools and movement models to better understand how invasive carp react to acoustic deterrents.

I did my PhD work at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln, where I was advised by Richard Rebarber and Brigitte Tenhumberg. My thesis was on a problem in mathematical ecology and modeling; specifically, I proved Perron-Frobenius-type results for integral projection models which have a non-compact operator.
